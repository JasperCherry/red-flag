






-------// Phase A: The "Blind Entry"
Total: 1 Screen

Screen 1: The Ambush. A full-screen interactive "event."

Interaction: User must make a split-second choice (e.g., "Accept/Deny" or "Click link/Ignore").

Exit: Regardless of choice, the screen transitions to Phase B with a "Did you make the right call?" teaser.






-------// Phase B: The "Bite-Sized Teach"
Total: 3 Screens

Screen 2: The Core Insight. Focuses on the "Why." Explain the psychology or the tech behind the attack.

Screen 3: The Red Flags. Visual screen. Highlighting exactly what to look for (3 bullet points max).

Screen 4: The Pro-Tip. A "Hack your own behavior" screen (e.g., The 10-second rule or a specific physical test).

Note: Use Scrolly-telling here to keep it feeling like one continuous flow rather than three "clicks."





-------// Phase C: The "Active Simulation"
Total: 4 Screens

Screen 5: The Lab (Visual). A "Spot the Fake" exercise.

Action: User must click the "glitch" in an image or video.

Screen 6: The Lab (Technical). A "Verification" exercise.

Action: Inspecting a URL, a sender’s address, or a file extension.

Screen 7: The Branching Chat (Part 1). The setup.

Action: A colleague/boss/stranger messages you. You pick a response.

Screen 8: The Branching Chat (Part 2). The resolution.

Action: The consequence plays out based on your choice. If you failed, you get a "Try again" loop or a "Learning Moment" popup.




-------// Phase D: The "Recap & Reward"
Total: 2 Screens

Screen 9: The Post-Game Analysis. * Content: "What we learned." 3 punchy bullet points.

Action: A "Download PDF Cheat Sheet" button for their desktop.

Screen 10: The Shield. * Content: Celebration screen.

Action: Animate the "Shield Badge" appearing. Shows the user's updated "Security Level" and a "Start Next Course" button.







-------// Why 10 Screens?

Production Consistency: You can build 10 standard React components and just swap the data (text/video/images) for all 20 courses.

User Expectations: Employees learn the "pattern." They know by Screen 5, they need to be ready to work, and by Screen 10, they are done.

Data Analytics: It’s easy for HR to see: "Most employees drop off at Screen 7 (The Chat)." This tells you exactly where the course is too hard or confusing.
